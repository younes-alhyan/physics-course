The International System of Units (SI) is the standard system used worldwide for measuring physical quantities.

  • It is based on seven base units, such as meter (m) for length, kilogram (kg) for mass, and second (s) for time.
  • Derived units are combinations of base units, e.g., m/s for speed.
  • SI units ensure consistency and universal understanding in science and engineering.
